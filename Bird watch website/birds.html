Birds of conservation concern in Ireland

There are a number of birds in Ireland whose numbers are in decline. Some of those with a high priority for conservations are:

Barn Owl

The barn owl is the most common owl and it is found in most parts of world. It has been a huge decline in Ireland and Europe in recent years.

Curlew

The curlew is recognisable by its long curved bill.

Curlews use their long bills to search for worms in mud or very soft ground.
